# Operating Agreement

<span class="tag">Key Provisions</span>

The Operating Agreement is the LLC's governing document. It overrides Pennsylvania's default LLC rules (15 Pa.C.S. Ch. 88) and establishes the bespoke arrangement the four members have negotiated.

---

## 1. Capital Contributions

Document each member's contribution in a **Schedule A** attached to the agreement:

- Cash amount (or agreed future contribution schedule)
- Valuation of non-cash contributions (equipment, IP, relationships)
- Whether additional capital calls require unanimous or majority consent
- Consequences for missing a capital call (dilution, conversion to debt)

> **PA Law:** Under 15 Pa.C.S. § 8842, a member's obligation to contribute is enforceable only if set forth in the OA.

## 2. Profit, Loss & Distributions

A **waterfall distribution** structure is recommended for this venture:

| Tier | What | To Whom |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | Return of capital | Members pro rata to capital accounts |
| 2 | Preferred return (e.g., 8%) | Members with capital invested |
| 3 | Promote split | Operating members (60%) / Capital member (40%) |
| 4 | Residual | Pro rata to ownership % |

**Tax:** The LLC files Form 1065, issues K-1s. Section 704(b) requires allocations have "substantial economic effect."

## 3. Management & Voting

**Manager-managed** is recommended: Partner 1 and Partner 2 as co-managers.

| Action | Vote Required |
|--------|---------------|
| Ordinary business | Manager discretion or majority |
| New member admission | Unanimous |
| Sale of substantially all assets | Unanimous |
| Debt above threshold | Supermajority (75%) |
| OA amendment | Unanimous |
| Dissolution | Unanimous |

**Deadlock resolution:** Mediation → either (a) Texas shoot-out or (b) binding third-party expert.

## 4. Transfer Restrictions

Keeping ownership inside the group is critical:

- **Right of first refusal** — must offer to company/members first
- **Consent requirement** — transfers to outsiders require unanimous approval
- **Tag-along** — minority can join a majority sale
- **Drag-along** — majority can force minority to join a sale

## 5. Buy-Sell Provisions

| Trigger | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| Death or disability | Company option to purchase at fair value |
| Voluntary withdrawal | Company buyout at fair value |
| Termination for cause | Buyout at discount (or forfeiture) |
| Valuation | Appraisal or agreed formula (e.g., EBITDA multiple) |

## 6. Non-Compete & Confidentiality

Given existing businesses, scope carefully:

- **Narrow scope** — only the venture's specific distribution business
- **Limited duration** — during membership + 1–2 years
- **Carve-outs** — existing businesses explicitly excluded
- **Confidentiality** — customer lists, supplier terms, pricing, financials

## 7. Dissolution

- **Voluntary:** Unanimous consent
- **Involuntary:** Bankruptcy, illegality, court order
- **Winding up:** Sell assets → pay creditors → distribute remainder per waterfall
- **Termination:** File Articles of Cancellation with PA
