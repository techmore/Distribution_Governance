# PA &amp; NJ Marijuana Context

<span class="tag">Regulatory Landscape</span>

Even for a distribution venture focused on non-plant-touching products, understanding the regulatory environment is essential — it dictates entity structure, banking, insurance, and compliance costs.

---

## Pennsylvania

**Governing Law:** Medical Marijuana Act (Act 16 of 2016)
**Regulator:** PA Department of Health — Office of Medical Marijuana

### License Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Grower/Processor | Cultivation and processing |
| Dispensary | Retail sale to patients |
| Clinical Registrant | Combined grow/dispense for health systems |
| Laboratory | Testing |

**Key facts:** Medical only (as of 2025, adult-use pending). No standalone "distributor" license exists. Vertically integrated grower/dispensary model is common. Advertising restrictions apply.

## New Jersey

**Governing Law:** CREAMMA (2021) — Cannabis Regulatory, Enforcement Assistance, and Marketplace Modernization Act
**Regulator:** NJ Cannabis Regulatory Commission (CRC)

### License Types (Adult-Use)

| Class | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Class 1 | Cultivator |
| Class 2 | Manufacturer |
| Class 3 | Wholesaler |
| **Class 4** | **Distributor** ← Most relevant |
| Class 5 | Retailer |
| Class 6 | Delivery Service |
| Class 7 | Consumption Area |

**Key facts:** Adult-use sales began April 2022. NJ has a specific **Distributor license (Class 4)** — authorizes transport and delivery between licensed businesses. Municipal opt-out allowed. No vertical integration required.

## Comparison: PA vs. NJ

| Factor | PA | NJ |
|--------|----|----|
| Adult-use | No (pending) | Yes (since 2022) |
| Medical | Yes | Yes |
| Distributor license | No | Yes (Class 4) |
| Vertical integration | Encouraged | Not required |
| Interstate transport | Prohibited | Prohibited |
| Tax | None on medical | 6.625% + municipal up to 2% |
| License cap | Yes | Yes (under review) |

## Federal Considerations

### Section 280E
Businesses trafficking Schedule I/II substances cannot deduct ordinary expenses. This applies to plant-touching operators. **Ancillary businesses** (consulting, logistics, non-infused products) may not be subject to 280E.

### SAFE / SAFER Banking Act
Pending federal legislation to allow cannabis businesses access to banking. Structure your entity to be bank-ready:

- Separate accounts per entity
- Robust compliance and reporting
- No direct cannabis revenue in non-licensed entities

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Separate PA and NJ entities** under a holding company to isolate regulatory risk
2. **If non-plant-touching**, regulatory burden is lower but not zero
3. **Insurance** — standard policies may exclude cannabis; specialized coverage is available
4. **Consult a cannabis attorney** — this area changes fast
