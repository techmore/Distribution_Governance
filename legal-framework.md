# Entity Framework

<span class="tag">Recommendation: LLC</span>

The single most important decision for a multi-member venture. The right entity determines liability protection, tax treatment, governance flexibility, and the ability to raise capital or exit.

## Entity Comparison

| | LLC | S-Corp | C-Corp | Partnership |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Liability** | ✅ Members protected | ✅ Protected | ✅ Protected | ❌ Personal liability |
| **Taxation** | Pass-through (default) | Pass-through | Double taxation | Pass-through |
| **Ownership classes** | Unlimited | One class only | Unlimited | Moderate |
| **Formality burden** | Low | High | High | Low |
| **Cannabis industry** | ✅ Most common | ⚠️ 280E issues | ⚠️ 280E issues | ❌ Not recommended |

## Why an LLC

### 1. Pass-Through Taxation
No entity-level tax. Profits and losses flow to members' personal returns. Early losses can offset other income (subject to basis rules).

### 2. Flexible Ownership
Create different membership classes — critical when four partners contribute unequal capital, time, and expertise. You can separate voting rights from economic rights, add preferred returns, and structure waterfall distributions.

### 3. Limited Liability
Members aren't personally liable for company debts — provided you maintain separate accounts, sign contracts in the company name, and avoid commingling funds.

### 4. Low Formality
No annual meetings, no minutes, no board. PA requires only a decennial report every 10 years.

### 5. Manager-Managed Option
Centralize day-to-day authority in one or two managers while reserving major decisions (debt, new members, dissolution) for all members.

## PA-Specific Requirements

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **File** | Articles of Organization (DSCB Form 15-8821) |
| **Where** | PA Department of State — online or mail |
| **Fee** | ~$125 |
| **Registered office** | Physical PA address required |
| **Ongoing** | Decennial report every 10 years ($70) |
| **Taxes** | Capital stock/franchise tax may apply |

## If Expanding to New Jersey

- Register as a **foreign LLC** (Certificate of Authority)
- File NJ Annual Report ($75)
- NJ Gross Income Tax on distributive shares
- NJ CRC licensing may require a separate NJ entity
