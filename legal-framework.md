# Legal Framework: Choosing the Right Entity

Selecting the proper legal structure is the single most important decision a multi-member venture can make. It affects liability, taxation, governance, fundraising, and exit strategy. Below is a comparison of the common options and why an LLC is likely the best fit for this distribution venture.

## Entity Comparison

| Factor | LLC | S-Corp | C-Corp | Partnership |
|--------|-----|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Liability** | Personal assets protected | Protected | Protected | Personal liability |
| **Taxation** | Pass-through (default) | Pass-through | Double taxation | Pass-through |
| **Ownership flexibility** | High — multiple classes, uneven splits | Restricted — one class only | High — unlimited classes | Moderate |
| **Formality** | Low to moderate | High (meetings, minutes, bylaws) | High | Low |
| **PA registration** | Articles of Organization (DSCB) | Articles + Form 2553 | Articles of Incorporation | No state filing required |
| **Management structure** | Member-managed or manager-managed | Board + officers | Board + officers | By agreement |
| **Cannabis industry fit** | Most common structure | Less common (280E impact) | Rare (280E impact) | Not recommended |

## Why an LLC Is the Strongest Option

### 1. Pass-Through Taxation
LLCs are not taxed at the entity level. Profits and losses flow through to the members' personal returns. This avoids the double taxation of a C-Corp and allows members to offset other income with early-stage losses (subject to basis and at-risk limitations).

### 2. Flexible Ownership & Economics
An LLC allows you to create **different classes of membership interests** — critical when four partners contribute unequal capital, labor, and expertise. You can structure:
- **Capital accounts** tied to cash contributions
- **Profit shares** that differ from ownership percentages
- **Waterfall distributions** (e.g., return of capital first, then preferred return, then promote split)

### 3. Limited Liability
Members are not personally liable for company debts or lawsuits — as long as corporate formalities are observed (separate bank accounts, proper contracts, no commingling of funds).

### 4. Minimal Formalities
Unlike corporations, LLCs are not required to hold annual meetings, keep minutes, or file annual reports beyond the PA Department of State's Decennial Report (every 10 years, with a small fee).

### 5. Manager-Managed Option
If the four members want centralized decision-making for day-to-day operations while retaining member-level votes on major decisions, a **manager-managed LLC** is ideal. The Operating Agreement defines exactly who manages what.

## PA-Specific Requirements

- **File** *Articles of Organization* (DSCB Form 15-8821) with the PA Department of State — Bureau of Corporations and Charitable Organizations
- **Online filing**: Available via the PA Business One-Stop Shop
- **Cost**: ~$125 filing fee
- **Registered office**: Must have a physical address in PA (not a PO box)
- **Decennial report**: Required every 10 years ($70 fee)
- **Annual taxes**: PA has a capital stock/franchise tax that may apply; consult a CPA

## NJ Considerations (Secondary Market)

If the venture expands to New Jersey:
- Foreign LLC registration required (Certificate of Authority)
- NJ Annual Report and fee ($75)
- NJ Gross Income Tax on members' distributive shares
- NJ Cannabis Regulatory Commission (CRC) licensing may require a separate NJ entity
